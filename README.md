## Professional Focus

My work focuses on **designing cinematic visual systems before image creation**.

Rather than treating AI as a prompt-driven image generator, I approach filmmaking as a **rule-based visual system**, where realism emerges from consistency across shots, not from isolated frames.

Key focus areas include:
- Cinematic visual continuity across sequences
- Light behavior and exposure logic across shots
- Spatial consistency and perspective stability
- Material response to light and motion
- Perceptual coherence during edits and cuts
- AI integration within professional film pipelines

---

## Core Working Philosophy

**Cinema is not created per image.  
Cinema is created per system.**

Single frames can appear visually convincing while failing completely once placed into motion or sequence. True cinematic realism is revealed only when visual rules survive across time, cuts, and camera changes.

This principle applies equally to:
- Camera-based cinematography  
- CG pipelines  
- Hybrid and AI-assisted workflows  

Tools change.  
Visual laws do not.

---

## Technical Working Methodology

My working method is **documentation-first and system-driven**, not trial-and-error or prompt-centric.

The process typically involves:

- Analyzing real-world photographic and cinematic references to understand lighting behavior, spatial relationships, and material response  
- Studying how continuity is preserved across shots in traditional film production  
- Documenting visual rules *before* generation or execution begins  
- Defining constraints for:
  - light direction and intensity  
  - exposure hierarchy  
  - spatial coherence  
  - scale and perspective stability  
  - perceptual consistency across edits  
- Designing repeatable visual systems instead of creating isolated images  
- Evaluating outputs at **sequence and edit level**, not as standalone stills  

This approach reduces instability, minimizes iteration cycles, and produces outputs that integrate naturally into professional post-production workflows.
